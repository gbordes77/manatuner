§01

Brand posture

Voice & the three laws.

ManaTuner is competitive Magic the Gathering, technical, generous. The visual language follows three rules. They are not negotiable.

— Law one

Authenticity over decoration.

The mana palette is the canonical Wizards colour set. Plains is a warm cream, Island is a deep blue, Swamp is near-black, Mountain is vivid red, Forest is forest green. They are signal, not flair. Do not pastel them. Do not desaturate them.

— Law two

Knowledge as premium.

The Library uses gradients normally reserved for high-tier products. Math content carries its own visual weight through monospace techTerm captions instead of corporate jargon. Karsten, Saito, Frank Karsten's tables: these are the brand.

— Law three

Quiet UI for a loud subject.

The chrome stays sober. Inter for body. Cinzel for the heads. Parchment for the surface. The mana symbols and the WUBRG hero gradient do the talking. Avoid generic SaaS purple gradients. The card art is the moodboard.

§03

Action hierarchy

Three tiers. Never invert.

A page carries at most one Tier 1. Gold = action. Blue → purple = knowledge. Solid brand blue = default. Two gradients are deliberate, not redundant; they encode the user's intent.

Tier 1 — Gold

Action.

Reserved for the single primary action of the page. The Analyzer's "Analyze deck" is the canonical example. One per viewport — anything more dilutes the signal.

--gradient-cta-premium · #E9B54C → #FFD700
Tier 2 — Knowledge

Exploration.

The Library and other knowledge surfaces. Blue → purple is the canon "exploration" pairing — distinct from gold so the user learns the difference between doing and reading.

--gradient-cta-knowledge · #0E68AB → #6A1B9A
Tier 3 — Default

Everything else.

Solid brand primary or an outline button. List actions, secondary navigation, dialog buttons, footer links. Carries no gradient — gradients are scarce on purpose.

--brand-primary · #1565C0

§05

Surfaces · radii

Parchment and paper.

Two surfaces carry the light theme. A parchment ground and a white paper card. Subtle borders, not heavy shadows; the depth comes from layering, not from dramatics.

— Background

Parchment

The base of every page. Warm, slightly aged, never pure white. Pairs with the radial mana glows at the corners of the viewport for atmosphere without weight.

--bg · #F5F3EE
— Card surface

Paper

The container for content. Pure white with a hairline rule, raised one micro-step above parchment. Use generous padding and let the type breathe.

--paper · #FFFFFF · border rgba(26,26,26,.08)
Radius sm 6 px Tags, inline code, micro-pills.
Radius md 8 px Toolbar chips, compact inputs.
Radius lg 12 px Buttons, inputs, base cards.
Radius xl 16 px Premium surfaces, modals.

Motion · easing

Lift, ease, respect.

All hover transitions ride the Material standard easing. Cards lift four pixels, buttons two. Mount animations cap at one per scroll viewport — chained reveals feel cheap. Hover the demos below.

prefers-reduced-motion is non-negotiable. Every animation in this system collapses to 0.01ms when the user has reduced motion enabled. The CSS guard is at the bottom of the stylesheet — never strip it. Motion is hospitality, not entitlement.

§08

The short list

Don'ts.

Constraints learned the hard way. Each rule has a cost behind it — a regression, a tester complaint, a category error. Read them, then design.

D—01
Pastel-ize the WUBRG mana colours.
They are signal, not flair. The moment they soften, the brand goes generic.
D—02
Use Swamp #150B00 as a button accent on dark mode.
Unreadable. The Commander track even keeps a #6B3FA0 override for the same reason.
D—03
Add a third CTA gradient.
Two is the limit. A third tier of gradient dilutes the entire hierarchy.
D—04
Promote techTerm captions to body weight.
They are discreet credits to Karsten and Bellman, not headlines competing with the H4 above them.
D—05
Default to generic purple gradients on a white surface.
AI-slop aesthetic. Reserve the blue → purple ramp for the Knowledge CTA and nothing else.
D—06
Strip the prefers-reduced-motion guard.
Motion is hospitality. The user's accessibility preference outranks every transition in this document.

End of edition.
